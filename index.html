Unlocking Housing

Open-source, local-first tools for discovering vacant and developable properties in Austria — built for bioregional communities, housing cooperatives, and people working to bring land back into use.

How it works Stay updated Project holon →

The problem it solves

Austria has significant stocks of vacant, underutilised, and potentially developable land — but finding it requires navigating fragmented cadastral databases, proprietary tools, and opaque administrative processes. The information exists; what’s missing is a way to work with it that is local, sovereign, and built for the communities who need it most.

Unlocking Housing is a suite of geo-mapping modules that runs on your own machine, connects to public Austrian spatial data sources, and produces actionable maps of where housing development may be possible — without sending your research to a third-party server.

Vacancy detection

Identify properties showing signs of vacancy or long-term non-use through spatial analysis of cadastral and planning data.

Bioregional context

Every property in its bioregional context — watershed, soil type, ecological connectivity — not just administrative boundaries.

Local routing

Valhalla-powered routing for access analysis: how reachable is a site by foot, cycle, or public transport? All computed locally.

Logseq integration

Research writes back to your local Logseq graph — every property investigation becomes a note in your personal knowledge base.

Who builds it and why

Unlocking Housing is the flagship project of the Bio pillar of EvoBioSys — the branch of the constellation concerned with living, biological, and bioregional systems. Where the Sys pillar builds the digital infrastructure and the Evo pillar supports human development, the Bio pillar works with the living substrate: land, housing, bioregions, and the material conditions of human settlement.

The project sits at the intersection of two urgent problems: the housing affordability crisis and the underuse of existing built and natural resources. Rather than building more, the starting question is: what is already here, and how can it be brought back into life?

The tools are designed primarily for housing cooperatives, community land trusts, municipal planners with limited budgets, and researchers working on Austrian housing policy — groups who need accurate spatial intelligence but cannot afford (or do not trust) commercial GIS platforms.

Built local-first

The entire stack runs on your own hardware. No data leaves your machine unless you explicitly export it. The architecture is designed to be extended, forked, and adapted to other European property markets with different data sources.

MapLibre GL JS SvelteKit Valhalla routing GeoJSON / PMTiles Austrian Open Data (AT) INSPIRE geodata Podman containers Logseq writeback QGIS-compatible

The monorepo is organised into focused modules — map-core, geo-layers-at, vacancy, bioregions, listings-matcher, and writeback — each independently usable and replaceable. Infrastructure is containerised via Podman for reproducibility across machines.
